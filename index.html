Who We Are

Iran and Its People

Iran, formerly known as Persia, is the world's oldest continuous civilization linked to a modern state, with over 5,000 years of history. Home to more than 90 million people, Iran has shaped human history through its rich culture — expressed in timeless poetry, music, art, and philosophy — leaving a lasting imprint on global civilization.

Persian people are widely known for their deep sense of hospitality, warmth, and generosity. They are commonly associated with a peaceful, patient, and respectful way of life, valuing dialogue, courtesy, and human connection.

A Legacy of Compassion

One powerful example of the Iranian people's humanity is welcoming approximately 116,000–120,000 Polish refugees during World War II. They arrived in Iran and were sheltered and cared for with compassion and dignity.

Source: US Holocaust Memorial Museum

The Truth

Islamic Republic ≠ Iran

While Iranians aspire to peace and respect for human life, the government (Islamic Regime) does not represent these values. It is a brutal and authoritarian regime that is built on extreme ideology to justify corruption and suppress the will of its people.

  • Rules through fear — using executions, torture, and censorship to silence dissent and crush basic freedoms
  • Systematically targets women, minorities, and activists
  • Exports instability and violence through proxy militias and extremist networks
  • The policies of this regime do not represent the culture, values, or character of its people
Bridges Between Nations

Iranian Diaspora & Canada

The Iranian diaspora in Canada has grown through several waves of immigration, shaped by political, social, and economic events. Many Iranians arrived as students, skilled professionals, entrepreneurs, and refugees, contributing extensively to fields such as engineering, medicine, academia, business, and the arts.

Today, the Iranian-Canadian community is among the most educated and civically engaged diaspora groups, deeply integrated into Canadian society. Iranians in Québec speak Persian (Farsi) alongside French and English, contributing to Québec's cultural, academic, and economic life while proudly preserving their vibrant cultural identity.

Iran-Canada Relationship

Before the establishment of the Islamic Republic, Iran–Canada relations were friendly, stable, and constructive. A notable moment was the official visit of Mohammad Reza Shah Pahlavi, the King of Iran, to Canada and Québec in 1965.

Relations deteriorated after 1979. Canada formally severed diplomatic relations with Iran in 2012, citing serious concerns over human rights abuses and support for terrorism.

Source: CBC News

A Vision for the Future

A free and democratic Iran can rebuild its strong historic relationship with Canada and unlock many positive opportunities, including expanded trade and investment in technology, agriculture, and industry, as well as the growth of tourism and cultural exchange.

History of Resistance

Four Decades of Protest and Repression in Iran

For more than 47 years, Iranians have endured the Islamic regime while persistently and peacefully fighting for their fundamental rights.

1979

Women's Protests Against Mandatory Hijab

  • First major protest against Islamic Republic policies
  • Women protest compulsory hijab
  • State response: repression and rollback of women's rights
1980–88

Mass Executions of Political Prisoners

  • Years of arrests, executions, political purges (1980–88)
  • Thousands executed in secret
  • "3-minute courts," no fair trials
Death toll: over 5,000 Human Rights Watch

Classified as crimes against humanity by Human Rights Watch

1999

Student Movement

  • Protests against restrictions on freedom of expression and press censorship
  • Violent attack on university dorms
  • Start of direct repression against students and youth
2009

Green Movement

  • Mass protests after disputed presidential election
  • Millions marched nationwide for peaceful protest
  • State response: repression, arrests, killings
  • Unrest continued for over a year
2017

Economic Protests Turning Political

  • Protests over economic grievances
  • Quickly became anti-regime
  • State response: extreme violence & repression
2019

Bloody November

  • Protests over fuel price increases
  • Brutally suppressed by the state
  • At least 1,500 killed in days
  • Deepened the divide between people and the regime
At least 1,500 killed Human Rights Watch
2020

Downing of Flight PS752

  • IRGC shoots down Ukraine International Airlines Flight PS752
  • 176 civilians killed (55 Canadians, 30 Permanent Residents)
  • Protests over state lies and impunity
  • State response: Arrests, intimidation, repression

2022

Woman, Life, Freedom

  • Nationwide protests after the killing of Mahsa Amini
  • Strong participation of women and youth
  • Against coercion, repression, injustice
  • Protests lasted nearly six months
  • Hundreds killed, including children and teenagers

Iran Sun and Lion National Revolution
